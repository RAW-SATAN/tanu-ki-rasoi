  • 🥭 Mango (Ramkela Aam)

How Tanu ki Rasoi Makes This Pickle Special

1

Farm Sourced

Fresh Ramkela mangoes sourced directly from farmers in Uttar Pradesh. Our didis use their traditional skills and regional recipes.

2

Washed & Cut

Mangoes are washed and uniformly chopped by our didis, then brined in salt and turmeric to draw out moisture.

3

Spice Mixing

Aromatic Indian spices are sautéed according to grandma's traditional Punjabi recipe and mixed with mango pieces.

4

Sun Fermented

Prepared pickle is stored in barnis and kept in the sun for fermentation, developing deep authentic flavour.

5

Lab Tested

Each batch undergoes lab testing to meet food safety and quality standards, ensuring hygiene and consistent taste.

6

Sealed in Barni

After approval, the pickle is filled into a barni, securely sealed, labelled, and begins its journey to your home.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which oil is used in this Punjabi Aam ka Achar? +

This pickle uses pure Mustard Oil (Sarso ka tel) which gives it the authentic Punjabi flavour and aroma.

What is the shelf life and how to store it? +

12 months from date of packaging. Store in a cool, dry place. After opening, refrigerate and always use a dry spoon.

Are there any preservatives in the pickle? +

No — zero chemical preservatives. The pickle is naturally preserved by salt, oil, turmeric and sun fermentation.

What can I eat with this Punjabi pickle? +

It pairs wonderfully with parathas, dal-chawal, curd rice, or simply with any roti. A small spoonful elevates any Indian meal.

What kind of jars are used for pickles? +

We use food-grade glass barnis with spill-proof hard foil covers that prevent leakage during shipping.

Our Story

🫙

From Tanu's Kitchen to Your Table

Tanu ki Rasoi was born out of a simple belief — that the best pickles in the world are made at home, with patience, love, and real ingredients. Tanu started making achar for her family using recipes passed down from her mother and grandmother in a small town in Uttar Pradesh.

When neighbors began asking for jars, and then their friends, and then strangers across the city — Tanu realised this wasn't just about pickle. It was about the taste of home, the smell of mustard oil in the morning, the crunch of mango on a summer afternoon.

The Tanu ki Rasoi Mission

We believe every Indian family deserves access to real, handmade achar — free from artificial preservatives, artificial colors, and shortcuts. Every jar is made in small batches, sun-fermented the traditional way, and lab-tested before it reaches your home.
